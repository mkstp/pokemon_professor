# GDD: Overview & Setting

---

## Overview

**Premise:** A student navigates a small computational linguistics institute and battles six professors — each a caricature of their research subfield — to pass their classes.
**Player fantasy:** The nerdy thrill of out-arguing your professors on their own turf.
**Tone:** Whimsical, warm, nerdy, affectionate.
**Target session length:** 20–30 minutes for one full run.

---

## World & Setting

The institute is a small, slightly eccentric technical university perched on a forested hill in rural New England. Its buildings range from weathered Gothic stone — including a genuine castle at the hill's crest — to utilitarian lab wings humming with GPU racks and cooling fans. The contrast between the ancient and the computational is part of the place's character: a medieval great hall down the corridor from a server room.

Outside, the campus is lush and unhurried. Elm trees and magnolias line the paths. A pond sits at the base of the hill, its walkway a favourite haunt for anyone avoiding office hours. In autumn the canopy turns amber and gold; the forest presses close on all sides. The whole place feels slightly apart from the world — a community of people who think very hard about very specific things, and are quietly delighted to be here.

Inside, the atmosphere is lived-in. The library has a mezzanine stacked with journals nobody has checked out since 2004. The graduate lounge smells of coffee and has one couch with a broken spring that everyone knows to avoid. The PhD dungeon is exactly what it sounds like: a basement office shared by too many people, lit by monitors, with a whiteboard that has not been fully erased in three years. The cafeteria is cheerful and slightly underfunded.

The institute's entire faculty, as far as the player can tell, specialises in computational linguistics.
